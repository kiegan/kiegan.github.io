About Me

Professional

I am currently pursuing my PhD in Statistics at Iowa State University.

I earned my BA from St. Olaf College in 2015, with majors in Mathematics and Spanish and a Statistics concentration.

I earned my MSc in Statistics from Iowa State University in December 2017. My creative component project was A User-Friendly U.S. Census Data Browser.

My research interests include reproducible data science pipelines, data visualization, statistical graphics, data journalism, statistical learning, and statistical applications in forensic evidence.

You can learn more about my professional activities by viewing my CV.

Personal

In my spare time, I enjoy playing board games, baking, reading, and spending time outdoors. I also really enjoy the New York Times crossword (when I have time). As of October 2019, my longest streak of completing the crossword was 61 days in a row!

I also really enjoy trivia, and am on a weekly trivia team with other Statistics graduate students. We have only won once, via tiebreaker.
